## A cascading global arrangement

On **17 and 18 June 2026**, the Brussels government resolved several stalled files as part of an arrangement negotiated between majority partners:

1. **Stations Plan**: approval of a plan endowed with **ten million euros per year** to upgrade the surroundings of the two main Brussels stations, **North** and **Midi**: deployment of around sixty cameras, enhanced cleaning by a private contractor, first measures from 2027 and, in the longer term, the transformation of the spaces under the tracks into public areas (see the Mobility and Spatial Planning cards).
2. **Brussels Environment**: agreement on the **appointment of the director-general** of the environmental administration (Leefmilieu Brussel), for a five-year mandate, after more than three years of blockage within the majority (see the Institutional card).
3. **Be Home premium**: **first-reading** approval of the **doubling** of this tax reduction for owner-occupiers, which would rise from **164 to 328 euros per year** in 2027 (around 200,000 households concerned). The text now goes for opinion to the Council of State and the social partners before a second reading (see the Housing and Budget cards).
4. **Drugs commissioner**: confirmation, on 16 June, of the **creation of a regional drugs commissioner** with a budget of **1.5 million euros per year** from 2027, tasked with coordinating security, prevention and health across the federal, regional and local levels (see the Security card and the Security Plan dossier).

## Two judicial and administrative milestones in the same week

- **Palais du Midi (metro 3)**: on **17 June** the Region issued the planning permit authorising the **partial dismantling** of the building, with strengthened heritage safeguards: this is the administrative lock that lifts for the digging of the missing-link tunnel. The ARAU association announces it intends to challenge the demolition (see the Metro 3 dossier).
- **Palais Stoclet**: on **18 June**, the **Constitutional Court annulled** the April 2024 ordinance that allowed the Region to impose public opening of properties listed by UNESCO, for lack of sufficient protection of the right to property and privacy. The Art Nouveau residence in Woluwe-Saint-Pierre will remain closed to the public.

## Institutional reading

The convergence of these decisions on the same day reflects a negotiated unblocking, four months after the government took office, on files that had been bogged down for years (appointment at Brussels Environment, station security). It comes as the majority remains under pressure on other fronts, notably the governance crises in social housing (Foyer anderlechtois, Saint-Josse social rental agency).
