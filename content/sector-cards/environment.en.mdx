## LEZ 2026: measured results, fines postponed

### 1 January 2026 phase

The **low-emission zone** now bans:

- **Euro 5 diesel** (registered 2011-2016)
- **Euro 2 petrol** (registered 1996-2001)

Approximately **400,000 vehicles** are affected nationally (Febiac).

### Fines in force since 1 July 2026

After a postponement announced on 17 April 2026 (the fines were not to enter into force on 7 June as originally planned), **LEZ fines for Euro 5 diesel and Euro 2 petrol vehicles entered into force on 1 July 2026**, at **€350**. Around **13,219 vehicles** already warned in previous years are affected. The annual LEZ pass (€350, social tariff €200) remains available as an alternative for driving legally.

Sources: [L'Avenir](https://www.lavenir.net/regions/bruxelles/2026/07/01/des-ce-mercredi-certaines-categories-de-voitures-diesel-et-essence-ne-peuvent-plus-rouler-a-bruxelles-sous-peine-damende-de-350-PRISPMALA5HWJFKSFGMQITOIRQ/) (1 July 2026); [La Libre](https://www.lalibre.be/belgique/mobilite/2026/07/01/voici-les-voitures-qui-ne-peuvent-plus-rouler-a-bruxelles-des-aujourdhui-sous-peine-dune-amende-de-350-FMDN2W2KRNEA5PAPTKXRJF5DMQ/); [BRUZZ](https://www.bruzz.be/actua/milieu/lage-emissiezone-vanaf-vandaag-krijgen-euro-5-diesels-een-boete-2026-07-01); [VRT NWS](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/nl/2026/07/01/lage-emissiezone-vanaf-vandaag-krijgen-euro-5-diesels-een-boete/).

## 2025 air-quality report: 6th year of NO2 compliance, WHO guidelines still missed (report of 15 July 2026)

The 2025 annual report from **Brussels Environment**, published on 15 July 2026, confirms the Region meets EU air-quality standards for nitrogen dioxide (NO2) for the **6th consecutive year**, and that the 2030 fine-particle targets are largely met. However, **every monitoring station in the Region still exceeds the levels recommended by the World Health Organization** (WHO 2025 guideline), particularly near major roads, where the situation remains problematic.

Sources: [DH](https://www.dhnet.be/regions/bruxelles/2026/07/15/lair-bruxellois-sameliore-mais-certains-axes-routiers-restent-problematiques-HJLVI5NIURGTNDMOF3TFSVCAXQ/) (15 July 2026); [L'Avenir](https://www.lavenir.net/regions/bruxelles/2026/07/16/lair-bruxellois-sameliore-mais-certains-axes-routiers-restent-problematiques-les-normes-oms-2025-ne-sont-pas-atteintes-J2R4YIQZJVGENC27CUJTR4273A/) (16 July 2026); [BX1](https://bx1.be/categories/news/la-qualite-de-lair-continue-de-sameliorer-a-bruxelles/) (15 July 2026).

## Aircraft noise: Evere joins inter-municipal legal front (24 April 2026)

During the week of **21-24 April 2026**, Evere's Municipal Council unanimously adopted a motion against aircraft noise from Brussels Airport, and an **also-unanimous amendment** committing Evere to **join the legal actions** already filed by **Schaerbeek, Koekelberg and Molenbeek** against the **RNP 07L** route (operational since summer 2025, up to 200 daily movements on certain axes).

See the cross-cutting dossier: [Brussels overflight](/en/dossiers/survol-bruxelles).

Source: [BX1](https://bx1.be/categories/news/nuisances-sonores-des-avions-evere-sunit-aux-communes-voisines/) (24 April 2026).

## PFAS: forever pollutants

Brussels Environment has identified **82 plots** contaminated with PFAS in the Brussels Region. The most severe case concerns the **Sicli site in Uccle** (former fire-extinguisher manufacturer), with levels reaching **1,058× the standard** in groundwater. TFA exceeds the EU norm of 500 ng/L (in force since January 2026) in Vivaqua's 6 reservoirs. The Environment Council calls for an integrated framework.

See the dossier: [PFAS](/en/dossiers/pfas).

Source: Brussels Environment / RTBF (2025-2026).

## Coalition agreement: transformed mechanisms

- **Renolution replaced** by **zero-interest loans** (€200M envelope through 2029) — the ~15,000 pending applications fall under this new regime
- **LEZ maintained**: schedule of future phases unchanged, annual pass €350 (social €200)
- **Brownfields**: Wiels, Avijl, Donderberg **preserved**; 18-month moratorium on Keyenbempt, Calevoet, Josaphat, Meylemeersch
- **Vivaqua-Hydria merger**: water management consolidated, **€180M** recapitalisation, Region 49.99%, **€30M** support
- **Roads**: +€10M in 2026 (AI maintenance, night works) within the budget voted on 27 March

**Open question**: the shift from grants (Renolution) to zero-interest loans changes the renovation incentive. Low-income households, who cannot borrow even at zero interest, risk being excluded. The impact on the ~75% poorly insulated buildings remains to be assessed.

## Support: Bruxell'Air

Brussels residents who give up their vehicle can benefit from the **Bruxell'Air premium**: a mobility budget covering public transport, car-sharing, bicycle or taxi.
