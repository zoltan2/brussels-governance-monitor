## Police Merger : Law Adopted, Simultaneous Appeal to the Constitutional Court

On **13 May 2026**, the **Chamber of Representatives** adopted in plenary the law merging the six Brussels police zones into a **single zone**, with **mandatory implementation before 1 January 2028** (no later than 18 months after publication in the Belgian Official Gazette).

The **transition budget** is set at **65 million EUR over five years** (initially 55 million, raised by 10 million in December 2025). The law also provides for a **single chief of police** and a **revision of the KUL funding norm** for police zones — first revision in twenty-six years.

Simultaneously with the vote, **Brulocalis** (on behalf of nineteen municipalities) and **four mayors** announced an **appeal to the Constitutional Court**.

## Unemployment Reform : 40,775 Brussels Exclusions Projected by ONEM

The **ONEM projection** transmitted this week to **Brulocalis** estimates at **40,775** the number of Brussels residents who would be excluded from unemployment benefits under the federal reform — out of **185,700 national exclusions**. This figure replaces the earlier estimate of around 42,000 that had been circulating since April 2026.

**Institutional reminder** : the unemployment reform is a federal competence. The Brussels Region has no direct lever, but its operators (Actiris, Bruxelles Formation) and the CPAS/OCMW will absorb the impact. **Actiris** counted **96,113 jobseekers** in March 2026, after a peak of 98,458 in January.

## Seniors : +34.8 % APA Beneficiaries Since 2021

The **Allowance for Assistance to the Elderly (APA)**, transferred from the federal level to COCOM since **1 January 2019**, rose from **6,523 Brussels beneficiaries** in January 2021 to **8,797** in December 2025 — an increase of **34.8 %** over five years.

The **total amount distributed** reaches **33.5 million EUR** in 2025, for an **average monthly amount** of **335.61 EUR**. The data is published by **stat.iriscare.brussels**.

The **« It takes a village »** programme has also been rolled out in **twenty-three Brussels nursing homes** under a Montessori approach ; first results are expected in 2026.

## Education : FWB Programme Decree and Postponed Rent Grid

The **Parliament of the Wallonia-Brussels Federation** has been examining since **10 May 2026** a **programme decree** of more than three hundred pages, translating approximately **500 million EUR in cumulative savings** by 2029.

Measures applicable to French-speaking Brussels schools :

- **Upper secondary education** : transition from **twenty to twenty-two hours** per week for teachers (4th-5th-6th years), without salary increase
- **DPPR** (Availability Prior to Retirement) : envisaged restriction from **four to two years** from 2027 (to be confirmed in second reading)
- **Free meals and school supplies** : reduction of envelopes
- **Tuition fees** : increase in higher education
- **Future qualified teachers** : salary revaluation **+5 %** from 2027

In parallel, the **SNPC-NEMS** (landlords' union) claims in a press release of **8 May** that the new Secretary of State for Housing has postponed the update of the **indicative rent grid** for 2026. Information **not confirmed** by the minister's office at this stage.

## Metro 3 and Overflight : Two Appeals, Two Timelines Under Pressure

On **12 May 2026**, **Secretary of State Ans Persoons** (Vooruit, Environment) announced her intention to propose to the Brussels government to **join the legal appeals** of the municipalities against the **RNP-07L air route**, via a **cessation motion**. Approximately **450,000 Brussels residents** are affected. Five municipalities are already in court : **Koekelberg, Molenbeek, Schaerbeek, Evere, Anderlecht**.

On **13 May**, during a new hearing of the **Metro 3 special committee**, the **Besix consortium** (candidate for the northern section, Gare du Nord → Evere) declared that the requested timeline of **six years** is **« unrealistic »**, and that at least **ten years** will be needed. The main technical difficulty : **soil freezing up to 82 metres** deep, a process never carried out at this scale anywhere in the world.

If the Region gives the green light to the northern section after the ten-year freeze provided for in the Government Policy Statement (i.e. from 2036), delivery would not be expected before **around 2046**.

## National Strike of 12 May : 43 % of Flights at Brussels Airport, STIB Network on Reduced Service

On **12 May 2026**, the national interprofessional strike severely disrupted Brussels mobility.

**Brussels Airport** operated **113 departing flights out of 263 (43 %)** and **146 arrivals out of 260 (56 %)**. **Brussels Airlines** cancelled **60 %** of its offering. **Charleroi** airport was closed entirely. This was the **ninth social-action-related disruption** at Brussels Airport since the start of 2025.

At **STIB/MIVB** :

- **Metro** : lines **1 and 5** only
- **Trams** (reduced service) : lines 4, 7, 8, 10, 82 and 92
- **Buses** (partial service) : ten lines (12, 14, 36, 46, 48, 53, 59, 71, 87 and 95)
- **Adapted traffic 10am-3pm** in the perimeter of the march

The demonstration gathered **40,000 people according to police**, **70,000 to 75,000 according to trade unions**, from Gare du Nord to Gare du Midi.

## Poverty : 37.3 % AROPE, COCOM/GGC Budget Approved in Committee

The Social file consolidates this week the **AROPE** indicator (At Risk Of Poverty or social Exclusion) at **37.3 %** of the Brussels population — more than **one Brussels resident in three** at risk of poverty or social exclusion.

On **12 May**, the **committee of the Joint Assembly** of **COCOM/GGC** voted by majority, against the opposition, the **first 2026 budget**. Statement by **Laurent Hublet** (Les Engagés, member of the Joint College in charge of the GGC Budget) :

> « There are no savings planned on the homelessness sector under the 2026 budget, but rather a slight increase in resources. »

**Institutional reminder** : the **COCOM/GGC** is the bicommunal Brussels authority competent for health, assistance to persons and social action. Its administration is **Vivalis** ; its internal research service is the **Health and Social Observatory**. The budget still needs to pass through the plenary of the Joint Assembly.

## Housing : 51 % of Brussels Rentals Without Compliant Smoke Detector

According to figures communicated on **12 May** by **Secretary of State for Housing Karine Lalieux** in committee, **51 % of Brussels rental properties inspected in 2025** are not compliant with the obligation of a **conforming smoke detector** — that is **766 deficiencies out of 1,494 inspections**, compared with **44 % in 2024**.

The equipment obligation is nonetheless **regional and long-standing**. The **DGHI** (Housing Directorate) carries out inspections and the control chain.
