## Two separate tracks, two crises of confidence

On **6 July 2026**, the parliamentary investigation committee on the Foyer anderlechtois closed its hearings after **143 hours** of cumulative work and **50 witnesses** heard. Two of the three rapporteurs resigned along the way (Les Engagés in late June, N-VA on 6 July), and the Brussels minister in charge of the VGC called the exercise a "farce". The vote on the final report is set for **17 July** in plenary session, with the **21 July** deadline remaining the formal end of the committee's mandate.

The next day, **7 July**, the State Secretary for Housing confirmed she is considering "very concretely" the appointment of a **government commissioner** to the Foyer anderlechtois itself — a mechanism provided for by article 78 of the Brussels housing code, distinct from the parliamentary investigation committee — after two formal warnings sent to the company (18 June, 1 July). The final decision rests with the Brussels government; the request was expected to be on the agenda of the SLRB/BGHM board of directors on **13 July**.

On **8 July**, the Ecolo group leader denounced ongoing "sabotage" within the committee: documents transmitted at the last minute, a cancelled meeting of the Parliament's expanded Bureau.
