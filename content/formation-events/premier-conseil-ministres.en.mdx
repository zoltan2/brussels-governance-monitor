## First cabinet meeting

The Brussels government held its first formal cabinet meeting on Thursday, February 19, 2026, five days after the swearing-in ceremony.

The minister-president set the tone upon arrival: *« On n'est pas en vacances. Il y a quand même urgence, il y a beaucoup de défis. »*

## Priority: the 2026 budget

The main stated objective is the adoption of the 2026 budget **before the end of March**. A vote in the Brussels Parliament is necessary to end the provisional twelfths regime in effect since the fall of the Vervoort government.

The budget minister confirmed that budgetary work has begun: *« Tout le monde se met au boulot. »*

## Balance between fiscal rigor and investment

The budgetary question opposes two approaches within the coalition:

- **Fiscal rigor**: the framework set by the government agreement provides for a return to balance in 2029
- **Protection of public services**: the PS affirmed that there would be *« pas d'austérité »*, insisting on maintaining public investment in social services, healthcare, and housing

The minister of Economy and Employment set the horizon for the mandate: *« On doit faire gagner Bruxelles, on a 1 200 jours. »*

## Next steps

- **Monday, February 23** — presentation of the Regional Policy Declaration (DPR) to Parliament
- **Wednesday, February 25** — vote of confidence in the Brussels Parliament
- **End of March** — adoption of the 2026 budget
