  - q: "Who manages mobility in Brussels?"

STIB, the public transport company, operates buses, trams and metros under regional oversight. The 19 municipalities manage their local roads and part of neighbourhood parking, within the framework set by the Region."

    a: "Good Move is the regional mobility plan adopted in 2020, built around traffic-calmed cells limiting through-traffic in neighbourhoods. The February 2026 Regional Policy Declaration provides for its replacement by a new Regional Mobility Plan; an evaluation of the current plan was due to be finalised by the end of 2026 before the successor's timeline and priorities are set."

## Rotation tariff on public EV charging points from 1 October 2026 (2 July 2026)

The Brussels government has given the green light to a **rotation tariff** on public EV charging points. From **1 October 2026**, any vehicle that remains connected for more than **six hours** between 9:00 and 22:00 will be charged **EUR 0.06 per minute** beyond that limit. The measure was proposed by the State Secretary for Energy (MR) and approved by the government; the State Secretary for Environment and Climate (Vooruit) also welcomed it. Grid operator **Sibelga** had announced as early as January 2026 that it was preparing this measure. The stated goal is to improve charging point availability as the number of electric vehicles keeps rising.

Source: BRUZZ (2 July 2026). Confidence: unconfirmed (press, single source).

## Metro vibrations in Woluwe and Auderghem: temporary agreement (2 July 2026)

Residents along metro lines **1 and 5** (Woluwe-Saint-Lambert, Woluwe-Saint-Pierre, Auderghem, Etterbeek, Anderlecht) have complained for years about noise and vibration. STIB identified the cause: **defective wheels** on the new **M7** trainsets, which deform while rolling. Wheel replacement began in **October 2025** and is expected to run until **end of September 2026**. Residents, financially backed by the municipality of Woluwe-Saint-Lambert and supported by Woluwe-Saint-Pierre, have brought legal action against STIB. A **"temporary"** agreement, valid until **30 September 2026** (the date STIB gives for completing the wheel replacement), has just been reached: M7 trainsets whose wheels have not yet been replaced will no longer run at night (22:00-6:00) or on weekends. This currently affects **4 trainsets**, a number set to shrink as wheels are replaced. STIB says it aims to "return to the comfort level from when only M6 trains ran on the line."

Source: La Libre (2 July 2026). Confidence: unconfirmed (press, single source).

## Car-free Sunday on 20 September 2026, the metro's fiftieth anniversary (23 June 2026)
The 2026 car-free Sunday will take place on 20 September, from 9:30 to 19:00, across the nineteen municipalities, making it the largest car-free zone in Europe that day. The date coincides with the fiftieth anniversary of the Brussels metro, whose first section was inaugurated on 20 September 1976. ## Coalition Agreement: Announced Commitments
The agreement of 12 February 2026 sets a major shift in mobility policy:

- **Good Move**: a **new Regional Mobility Plan** will succeed it, with smaller zones (school perimeters)
- **Metro 3**: **frozen for 10 years**, replaced by a tram completing the loop of the existing network
- **Tram 15** (Gare du Nord -- Tour & Taxis): maintained
- **STIB construction sites**: Constitution and Palais du Midi continue
- **Car-free Sunday**: a second Sunday per year
- **LEZ**: maintained -- **annual pass at EUR 350** (reduced rate EUR 200), fine per violation reduced from 350 to **EUR 80**, 4-fine annual cap removed
- **STIB fares**: preferential youth/senior rates maintained
- **Contested bollards**: removal

The 10-year freeze on Metro 3 is the most structuring decision: it ends the project as conceived (extension to Bordet, EUR 4.76 billion) and redirects investment towards the tramway.

## Road safety — 20 killed in 2025 (doubled)

On 10 March 2026, the Vias Institute publishes road fatality figures for Belgium in 2025. **Brussels recorded 20 traffic deaths in 2025**, compared with 10 in 2024, 10 in 2023 and 10 in 2022 — a doubling in one year. The trend runs counter to the other two regions: Flanders dropped from 248 to 234 deaths (−6%), Wallonia from 197 to 191 (−3%).

At the national level, electric scooters are a warning signal: **13 killed in 2025** (up from 4 in 2024). Pedestrian deaths reached a historic low (52, down from 70 the previous year). Overall severity is declining: 12 killed per 1,000 injury accidents in 2025, versus 16 per 1,000 in 2016.

This doubling in Brussels directly contrasts with the **Vision Zero** target set out in the Dilliès government's DPR (chapter 3: "zero road deaths on Brussels roads"). No specific implementation measures have been announced to date.

A **written parliamentary answer** from the Minister of Mobility (in response to a question from regional MP Ilyas El Omari, Team Fouad Ahidar) states that since early 2024, **7 fatal accidents** involved an STIB vehicle — the victim being a pedestrian in every case. 2025 breakdown: of the 20 fatalities, 6 were pedestrians, 3 cyclists and 4 scooter users. The MP is calling for "a comprehensive review" of the road safety plan.

Source: Vias Institute (police data), BRUZZ (10 March 2026).

## LEZ: regulatory gap with 6 days to go before 1 April (26 March 2026)

With 6 days until the next phase of the LEZ calendar takes effect (**1 April 2026**), the regulatory framework is not finalised. The government has announced a **transitional system**, but this remains an "informal agreement in principle" — **no official text (decree) has been published** to date.

## Local improvements April 2026: Place Sainctelette, Colonel Bourg, « Spanish lights »

In early April, three local mobility files progressed — each illustrating in its own way the coordination (or friction) between the Region and the municipalities.

**Place Sainctelette — traffic redirected to the southern bridge (14 April 2026)**

As part of the overall renovation of the square and the bridge over the canal, which began in 2025, Brussels Mobility launched a new works phase on **14 April 2026**: the **northern bridge** is under restoration, all motor traffic is redirected to the **southern bridge** — one lane per direction maintained. **Tram 51 remains suspended** on this section. **Rue Colonel Bourg (Schaerbeek) — about a dozen parking spaces removed**

Also in early April, **Brussels Mobility removed about a dozen parking spaces** on Rue Colonel Bourg, a regional road near the RTBF site, to widen cycling infrastructure. The stated reason: safety, following the **death of a cyclist on 11 July 2024** on this same street. The decision provoked a **public tension** between the **Regional Minister of Mobility (Groen)** and the **PS alderman for Mobility of Schaerbeek**, the latter contesting the lack of consultation with the municipality. **« Spanish lights » in Auderghem — 8 devices, Region sceptical**

Auderghem now has **8 « Spanish lights »**: traffic lights equipped with a camera that measures vehicle speed over 50 to 200 metres upstream and **automatically turns red** in case of excess speed. The first two were installed in 2024 near Saint-Hubert College and the Royal Athenaeum. **They are not radars**: no fine is generated, no driver is identified. **Auderghem says it is satisfied** with results observed near schools. **Brussels Mobility is sceptical**: according to the regional administration, effectiveness is questionable (drivers slow down only in the detection zone then accelerate, or run red lights) and the system is poorly suited to coordinated traffic management in Brussels — a classic repressive radar would remain more effective in their view.

## Per-kilometre lorry charge: indexation on 1 July 2026

On 26 May 2026, **Viapass** — the body that coordinates Belgium's per-kilometre lorry charge — announced the regional tariffs applicable from **1 July 2026**. In Brussels, as last year, the change is limited to **indexation**. The range is structurally unchanged: from **€0.017/km to €0.267/km** on Brussels motorways, and from **€0.024/km to €0.390/km** on local and regional roads (excl. VAT).

The charge, in force since April 2016, applies to **lorries over 3.5 tonnes** and depends on weight, Euro emission standard and region. **Wallonia** is increasing its tariff by an average **+5.7%** this year. **Flanders** is adding, for the first time, a **CO2 parameter** (five emission classes, zero-emission vehicles exempt), in line with a European obligation already applied by Germany, Austria and Denmark. Brussels has not introduced this CO2 parameter at this stage.
