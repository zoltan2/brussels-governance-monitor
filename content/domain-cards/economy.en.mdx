## Orange Belgium leaves Evere for Diegem (18 July 2026)

Orange Belgium confirmed, on **18 July 2026**, its intention to leave its current headquarters in **Evere** (around **1,400 employees** affected) to relocate to **Diegem**, in Flemish Brabant, outside the Brussels-Capital Region. The move is planned for **late 2028**.

The information was first revealed by L'Echo, before being officially confirmed by the operator. Orange justifies the choice by the end of its lease in Evere and by the current building's mismatch with current technical and environmental standards and with new ways of working. The company explicitly states that this relocation is not motivated by a move to Flanders for tax reasons or any other reason.

Sources: [BX1 (18 July 2026)](https://bx1.be/categories/news/orange-belgique-quittera-evere-pour-installer-son-siege-a-diegem-en-2028/), [DH (18 July 2026)](https://www.dhnet.be/regions/bruxelles/2026/07/18/un-nouvelle-grande-boite-quitte-bruxelles-pour-la-flandre-orange-va-demenager-devere-vers-la-peripherie-bruxelloise-TSQFUVFK2NARBCF5KHDCCKOLF4/). Confidence: official (confirmed directly by the company).

## Municipal office tax: City of Brussels ruled in violation of the equality principle (22 June 2026)

The Dutch-speaking court of first instance of Brussels ruled, on **22 June 2026**, that the **City of Brussels'** tax regulation on office space (assessment years 2022 to 2025) violates the **constitutional principle of equality**, for lack of objective justification to distinguish office space from space used for other purposes. The disputed tax assessment in this case was cancelled; according to the analysis by the Tiberghien law firm, the issue does not appear to have been corrected in the new regulation applicable for the 2025-2032 period.

This tax, specific to the municipality of the City of Brussels and distinct from any regional taxation, has risen sharply since 2021: from **EUR 9.58/m²/year** to **EUR 24/m²/year** in 2025-2026, an increase of approximately **150% over five years**, including a 13.4% increase voted by the municipal council in June 2025 (from EUR 17.85 to EUR 24/m²). This trend concerns only the municipality of the City of Brussels; BGM has not verified whether comparable trajectories exist in the 18 other Brussels municipalities.

Sources: [Tiberghien, legal analysis](https://www.tiberghien.com/fr/4641/reglement-taxe-de-la-ville-de-bruxelles-contraire-au-principe-d-egalite), [Aximas, tax analysis](https://www.aximas.com/fr/blog/lexplosion-de-la-taxe-sur-les-bureaux-a-bruxelles.html). Confidence: official for the ruling (analysis by a tax law firm on a court decision); estimated for the quantified tax trajectory (non-official source, not cross-checked against the text of the municipal tax regulation).

## Economic context (February 2026)

### First Council of Ministers (19 February)

The Dilliès government held its first Council of Ministers on 19 February 2026. The regional budget, absent for over 600 days, was approved on 5 March and filed to Parliament on 6 March 2026 (deficit reduced to EUR 957 million).

### Construction: leading indicator of the regional economy

The construction sector, a barometer of economic activity, is experiencing a pronounced crisis:

- **−1,402 construction companies** in 2024 — first net loss in 10 years (Embuild)
- **1,443 bankruptcies** in construction in H1 2025 — all-time record, +10% year-on-year
- **9,467 housing units** authorised in Q1 2025 — the lowest level since the start of Statbel statistics (2012)
- In Brussels: **405 construction bankruptcies** in 2024, second most affected sector
- Average time to obtain a building permit in Brussels: **~7 years**

The Urban Free Zone (Port + Audi Forest) provided for in the RPD aims to counter this trend, but experts do not anticipate a recovery before 2027.

Sources: Embuild, "record number of bankruptcies in construction" (2025); RTBF, "bankruptcies rising, permits at lowest" (2025).

## RPD commitments

The Regional Policy Declaration of 13 February 2026 devotes a chapter to the economy and international attractiveness of Brussels.

### Urban Free Zone

The government plans the creation of an **Urban Free Zone** at two sites:

- **Port of Brussels** — logistics and industrial vocation
- **Audi Forest site** — industrial reconversion after the factory closure

Three levers are planned:
- **Tax lever** — reduction of property tax and registration rights within the zone
- **Administrative lever** — single Fast-Track window for businesses
- **Human lever** — dedicated Actiris cell for jobs in the zone

### Attractiveness and innovation

- Positioning Brussels as the **capital of artificial intelligence**
- Hub.Brussels: continued international economic missions
- Innoviris: integrated process with finance&invest.brussels
- GovTech hub (Schuman, Mediapark, Kanal)

### Culture and major projects

- **Kanal** — museum opening with new governance and management contract
- **Brussels Expo CONFEX** (CONference + EXhibition) at the Heysel, funded via NEO
- Support for the nightlife sector and events
- Zinneke Parade and BXL 2030: support confirmed

### Social economy and entrepreneurship

- Strong commitment to social economy
- Support for female entrepreneurship and diversity

### Sport

- **RUSG** — stadium at Bempt (Forest)

## Tourism: visit.brussels closes its last tourist office (30 June 2026)

On **Tuesday 30 June 2026**, **visit.brussels** closed its tourist welcome office on the **Grand-Place**, housed in the Town Hall since the 1960s (a remnant of the "Belgique Joyeuse" era, around Expo 58 and the Atomium). Brussels now has **no physical tourist office left at all**: the other office, on Place Royale, had already been closed since 3 April 2026 (see the "Tourism: Visit Brussels weakened by austerity" section below).

Staff and the joint union front held a farewell moment and waved off passing tourists themselves. Visit.brussels confirmed to BRUZZ that its walk-in and reception function has been entirely scrapped, by decision of the Brussels government.

This closure follows directly from the **redundancy plan the government approved on 26 June 2026** (37 voluntary departures out of 159 full-time equivalents, see above) and from the budget cuts imposed on the agency: **EUR 3 million** this year, rising to **EUR 6 million** by 2029, according to visit.brussels. In mid-June 2026 it had already emerged that the agency would cut 37 posts (about a quarter of its workforce); the government maintains that no forced layoffs will be necessary.

A union representative, quoted by BRUZZ, says tourists will now have to rely on Google or search engines for information; he regrets the loss of an "artisanal", personalised service and says Brussels loses "a piece of history". These characterisations reflect the union representative's own opinion, not an assessment by this document.

Sources: [BRUZZ (30 June 2026)](https://www.bruzz.be/actua/economie/visitbrussels-sluit-toeristisch-kantoor-op-de-grote-markt-door-besparingen-2026-06-30). Confidence: official (closure and budget cuts confirmed by the agency itself); unconfirmed (the union representative's characterisation).

## 2025 growth: construction carries activity (IBSA)

In its economic note of 18 June 2026, the **IBSA** reports that Brussels **market activity** grew by **+0.4%** in 2025. **Construction** is the main driver, at **+6%** (building construction and property development **+10%**, specialised construction works **+4%**). **Accommodation** also rose sharply (**+10%**). Conversely, **business services** are flat (no growth) and **retail trade** remains below its pre-pandemic levels.

This dependence on construction contrasts with the deterioration of the labour market observed in the same year (see the Employment card: decline in salaried employment, temporary agency work at its lowest).

Source: IBSA, relayed by L'Avenir and La DH Bruxelles (18 June 2026).

## Social Summit: 17 shared priorities with the social partners (11 June 2026)

On **11 June 2026**, the Brussels government and the social partners gathered within **Brupartners** (the Brussels social-partner council of employer organisations and trade unions) signed a **socio-economic protocol agreement** setting **17 shared priorities** for the legislature (horizon 2029), structured around **seven axes**: mobility, employment, urban planning and land use, economy, environment, social affairs and health.

Examples of cited priorities: a renewed 2030 strategic vision of territorial development (housing, environment, economic development), a regional mobility plan 'integrating socio-economic stakes', stronger support for jobseekers, an '**investment shock**' through the simplification of administrative procedures, and support for care structures with a guarantee of universal access to healthcare. Each minister or state secretary must convene **by September 2026** a steering committee tasked with specifying objectives, deliverables and timetable.

The Minister-President, who coordinates the Social Summit, presents the government agreement as a 'compass' to be tested 'against realities on the ground'; the chair of Brupartners insists on co-construction and in-depth social dialogue in the implementation.

Source: La DH (11 June 2026). Confidence: unconfirmed (protocol not published at this stage).

## Office market: The Muse sold for 100 million EUR (9 June 2026)

Developer **Immobel** is selling **The Muse** office complex (rue d'Arlon, European quarter, 10,500 m², renovation completed in April 2026) to Cypriot company Easternmed Real Estate Capital for **100 million EUR** — according to BRUZZ and Trends-Tendances, the largest Brussels office transaction since 2022. The tenant, the **European Defence Agency**, has committed for fifteen years. Trends-Tendances adds nuance: the transaction does not signal 'a clear recovery' of the office market. Confidence: unconfirmed.

## Federal impact: hotel VAT increase (1 March 2026)

The federal government has decided to raise the **VAT on hotel stays from 6% to 12%** effective 1 March 2026. This measure, part of the federal budget agreement, directly affects the Brussels hotel sector — Brussels accounts for the majority of the country's tourist and business overnight stays.

The Brussels Hotels Association estimates the impact at approximately **+EUR 8.50 on a EUR 150 room night**. The federation denounces a "cocktail of taxes in 2026" combining this increase with other levies.

In parallel, the VAT on soft drinks served in hospitality establishments drops from 21% to 12%, a partial relief for the restaurant sector.

The regional RPD provides for **free zones** and attractiveness measures to partly offset these increased federal charges.

## Data Centre and AI Infrastructure

Belgium is attracting major digital infrastructure investments: **Google** (~EUR 5 billion in Saint-Ghislain), **Microsoft** (>EUR 1 billion in Mont-Saint-Guibert). In the Brussels Region, the **KevlinX BRU01** data centre (Neder-Over-Heembeek, 32 MW) has been operational since January 2026. Its estimated consumption (~168 GWh/year) would represent ~3.7% of regional electricity. The RPD positions Brussels as an 'AI capital' without a specific urban planning framework for data centres.

Source: KevlinX, press, RPD 2026.

## Defence cluster and dual-use opening (March 2026)

On 12 March 2026, at the **Bedex** exhibition (Brussels European Defence Exhibition & Conference, 200+ exhibitors, 35 international delegations), the Brussels Economy Minister announced two decisions:

1. **Creation of a technology cluster dedicated to defence** — a network of Brussels companies in the security-defence sector, building on Brussels' position as NATO headquarters and the site of the new Belgian Defence headquarters
2. **Opening of finance&invest.brussels to defence and dual-use technologies** — investments in defence sectors and dual-use solutions (civilian and military) were previously excluded from the regional investment strategy. The Region holds approximately 70% of finance&invest.brussels

Hub.brussels organised a collective stand at the exhibition showcasing Brussels companies in the sector: Macq, NVISO, Bretholz Solutions, Proximus NXT, Hozint, among others.

Sources: BX1, DH (12 March 2026).

## Tourism: Visit Brussels weakened by austerity (March 2026)

The 2026 Brussels regional budget deprives **Visit Brussels** of approximately **EUR 6 million** in operational resources (~1/5 of the annual budget), a direct consequence of regional austerity measures. According to unions, the austerity trajectory would bring subsidies from **EUR 22 million down to EUR 8 million by 2029**. The agency, which employs **160 people** (of whom approximately **90 jobs are at risk**), is responsible for promoting leisure and business tourism in the capital, a sector representing **35,000 direct and indirect jobs**.

**Immediate consequence**: the **Iris Festival 2026** is reduced to **one day** (Saturday only, Sunday cancelled). The 2025 edition had attracted approximately **100,000 visitors**. The director of **Square Brussels** estimates the impact at **30-35% less activity**, describing the measure as "counter-productive in the long term".

This cut comes on top of the **hotel VAT increase** (6% to 12%, 1 March 2026) and the **EUR 3 million/year** savings imposed on Hub.brussels (Brussels Houses Milan/Barcelona threatened), forming a triple impact on Brussels' attractiveness.

**Cultural sector on alert**: the bilingual network **RABKO** (120+ cultural institutions) addressed an open letter to the Brussels Parliament on 19 March 2026, denouncing three threats: the abolition of "Image de Bruxelles" subsidies, the ACS status reform and the Visit Brussels cuts.

**Public mobilisation**: a petition gathering **17,000+ signatures** circulated against the cuts, and a rally by staff and supporters was planned for **25 March 2026** outside the Visit Brussels offices.

## Brussels Houses threatened with closure (March 2026)

Hub.brussels, the Brussels agency for international business support, must achieve savings of **EUR 3 million per year** (EUR 6 million by 2029) on its international operations, a direct consequence of the 2026 austerity budget.

Approximately **9 of 32 foreign offices** of Hub.brussels are at risk of closure, including the **Brussels Houses** in **Milan** and **Barcelona** — partnerships with Visit.brussels for economic and tourism promotion. The definitive list of closures has not yet been confirmed.

**Ministerial confirmation (27 March 2026)**: Economy Minister **Laurent Hublet (Les Engages)** confirmed the cuts on BX1, stating: "At some point, we have to make efforts." He cited Cuba as an example of a questionable priority. The international network budget stands at **EUR 10.2 million**. Planned savings reach **EUR 3 million** from 2026, then **+EUR 1 million/year** until 2029 — totalling **EUR 6 million** in savings (⅔ of the budget). In addition, **EUR 1.4 million** in linear cuts were imposed by Budget Minister De Smedt (Anders).

The **33 offices** abroad face near-total closure. The government claims to want to preserve "more than half" of the network. Concerns centre on contracts already signed (participation in international fairs) and the impact on exporting Brussels businesses. This measure adds to the cuts imposed on Visit.brussels, forming a triple shock to Brussels' international attractiveness.

Sources: BX1 (27 March 2026), La Libre, La DH (26 March 2026). Confidence: official (ministerial confirmation).

## Innoviris freezes all new R&D projects (March 2026)

The regional research and innovation fund **Innoviris** has decided **not to launch any calls for proposals in 2026**, following a budget cut of **~20%**. Ongoing projects from 2025 will continue, but no new projects will be funded.

**Impact:**

- **Brussels universities** (ULB, VUB, UCLouvain Saint-Louis) lose an essential funding source for applied research
- **Innovative SMEs** are cut off from the main regional R&D support mechanism
- The freeze contradicts the RPD's ambition to position Brussels as an "AI capital"
- **Quadruple austerity shock** on attractiveness: Innoviris (R&D), Hub.brussels (international network), Visit Brussels (tourism) and the federal VAT increase (hotel sector)

The decision is all the more notable because the regional R&D budget (EUR 2,532.9M, 2.45% of GDP) already falls below the European target (3%) and the private sector accounts for 71.9% of researchers — the public lever was Innoviris' distinguishing feature.

Source: BRUZZ (27 March 2026).

## Fire on the Kanal construction site (6 July 2026)

Late in the day on **6 July 2026**, a **fire** broke out in a **technical room** under the roof of the Kanal construction site, covering an area of around **400 m²** — in the section intended for **Kanal Architecture** (formerly CIVA), separate from the future museum's exhibition spaces. Brussels firefighters intervened and brought the fire under control within just over an hour, with clean-up and securing operations continuing into the evening. **No one was injured**: the building was unoccupied at the time, and no artworks were present in the affected section, which was still under construction.

The cause of the fire is not known at this stage. **Structural engineers** are assessing whether the building's structure was affected. A meeting between the contractor and the Kanal Foundation was held on 7 July to evaluate the consequences for the project; its outcome has not been communicated. **No cost estimate, no insurer, and no confirmed impact on the 28 November 2026 opening date** appear in the press so far.
