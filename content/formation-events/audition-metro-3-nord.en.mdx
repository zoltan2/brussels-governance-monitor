## Context: 18th hearing of the special commission

The **Brussels Parliament's Special Metro 3 Commission** has been holding hearings for several months on cost overruns (+477%, from less than €1bn to €4.76bn according to the Court of Audit), decision-making and project viability.

The **18th session** took place on **Thursday 23 April 2026**. Main witness: **Henri Ernst**, CEO of **Galère SRL** (part of the **SM Progrès** consortium, appointed by Beliris to excavate the underground cavity beneath the North Station).

## Statement: the northern section is compromised

Henri Ernst: "We assume there is **little chance that the project [under the North Station] will proceed**."

**Findings from the hearing:**

- **Beliris recently asked** the consortium to complete only the **surface urban redesign around** the station (rue du Progrès, rue d'Aerschot) — no longer the underground cavity.
- Unforeseen **ground conditions** (water ingress) forced the abandonment of the original method.
- A **new V.E.R.T. technique** would be required, estimated at **+EUR 75.119M** on top of the **EUR 22.56M already spent** — with no feasibility guarantee.
- The site has been **idle for several years**; SM Progrès teams have left the site.

## Institutional impact

- The **northern branch of Metro 3** (which was to reach Bordet via a tunnel connected to the North Station cavity) is now suspended **beyond** the 10-year freeze set in the RPD.
- **Surface works** can nevertheless resume.
- The case raises questions about the **decision chain** Beliris ↔ Region ↔ Federal on the future of the northern route.
