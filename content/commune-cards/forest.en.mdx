## An unprecedented left-wing coalition

Forest has approximately 56,000 inhabitants and is marked by the formation of an unprecedented coalition in Brussels: **PS-PTB-Ecolo**. The MR-DéFI list, which came first with 10 seats, was excluded from power. Charles Spapens (PS) is mayor.

## Transparency: 5 out of 6 criteria

Forest achieves a good score. The **budget** and council **minutes** (archived PDFs) are published online, the council is broadcast via **livestream** on YouTube, the **mandate registry** is accessible, and the municipality has a **Forest Citizen Council** facilitated by Dreamocracy. No dedicated **Open Data** portal.

## Citizen participation: Forest Citizen Council

Forest established a **Forest Citizen Council** with the help of Dreamocracy, allowing residents to participate directly in municipal decisions. A citizen initiative right with public consultation completes the framework.

## Audi Forest site: reconversion underway

The former Audi plant site (**54 hectares**) is the subject of a major reconversion project. The regional government approved the creation of an **urban free zone** to attract investors. **Heylen Warehouses** has been selected for the site's logistics management. On the social side, **49% of former workers** have been reclassified. The municipal budget reflects this challenge: revenues of EUR 160M against expenditures of EUR 154M.

## Union Saint-Gilloise stadium at Bempt: favourable opinion, Forest abstains (30 June 2026)

Following a public inquiry held from 13 May to 9 June 2026, the concertation commission issued a favourable opinion with numerous conditions (mobility, parking, noise, water management, biodiversity, safety) on **30 June 2026** for the new Union Saint-Gilloise stadium project at Bempt (**15,881 seats**). The municipality of Forest **abstained** in the vote, citing flood risks, the impact on mobility, nuisance for local residents, and the need to relocate municipal sports facilities; it is demanding regional budget commitments in return. No permit has yet been issued.

## Waste collection crisis (May 2026)

In early May 2026, several Forest neighbourhoods (including the area around the stadium, Place Saint-Denis, the border with Saint-Gilles, then Saint-Antoine, Pont de Luttre and Bempt) did not have their waste collection rounds carried out, due to a regional hiring freeze at Bruxelles-Propreté. The alderman for Cleanliness, Simon De Beer (PTB), called for retired staff to be allowed to be replaced.

Sources: L'Avenir, DH (6 July 2026, stadium); RTBF (13 May 2026, public inquiry); RTBF, DH (7 May 2026, waste collection).
