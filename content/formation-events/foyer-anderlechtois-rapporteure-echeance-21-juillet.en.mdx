## An inquiry committee under pressure

On **30 June 2026**, the rapporteur of the parliamentary inquiry committee on the Foyer anderlechtois, a **Les Engagés** lawmaker, announces she is stepping down from that role. She refuses to co-sign a final report she considers impossible to produce "with the necessary rigour" given the pace imposed on committee members: hearings of **10 to 12 hours**, six days a week, Saturdays included. She also points to witnesses who appeared "clearly restrained", after coaching organised by the Foyer anderlechtois's lawyer and a coaching firm hired at the company's expense. Her party states this resignation does not call into question the need to shed light on the case.

On **3 July**, the hearing of the Foyer anderlechtois's chairman, removed since 15 June, ends in under two hours: after an opening statement, he invokes his right to remain silent, saying he trusts the justice system and does not wish to answer lawmakers' questions because of the ongoing criminal investigation.

The same day, the majority (MR-PS-Les Engagés-Groen-Vooruit-CD&V) rejects, by **40 votes to 25**, an opposition request to extend the committee's work, after a lawmaker (N-VA) called the workload untenable. The **21 July 2026** deadline, set when the committee was created on 5 June, is therefore maintained.
