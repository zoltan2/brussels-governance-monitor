## A stable coalition renewed

Berchem-Sainte-Agathe is a small municipality of 25,800 inhabitants led by Christian Lamouline (Les Engagés). The Engagés-PS-Ecolo coalition was renewed for the 2024-2030 term with 21/29 seats.

## Transparency: 5 out of 6 criteria

The **budget** and council **minutes** (via the EDITORIA platform) are published online, the council is broadcast via **livestream** on YouTube (channel 1082berchem), the **mandate registry** is accessible, and the municipality has a dedicated **citizen participation** platform. No **Open Data** portal.

## Citizen participation: dedicated platform

Berchem-Sainte-Agathe has a digital participation platform (particip.berchem.brussels) inviting citizens to co-build the municipality's future.

## Recent developments

The municipality will welcome its **first secondary school** in September 2026, filling a historic gap. The **Cité Moderne** (Art Deco architectural heritage) is undergoing a renovation programme. The 2026 budget was adopted **with no tax increase**. The CPAS has deployed a **chatbot** to improve services for beneficiaries.

## Aircraft noise: the municipality joins the regional action (29 June 2026)

The Berchem-Sainte-Agathe municipal council voted unanimously on **29 June 2026** to join the environmental cessation action launched by the Brussels Region against the intensive use of Brussels Airport's **07L** landing runway, which flies over north-west Brussels. According to the municipality, up to 96% of landings on this route violated Brussels noise regulations in April 2026 — a figure not yet corroborated by an independent source. This step is part of a long-running municipal file: a 2023 motion, a 2024 appeal.
