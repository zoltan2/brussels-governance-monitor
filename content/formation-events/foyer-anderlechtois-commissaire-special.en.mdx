## A vote and an appointment, in 24 hours

On **Thursday 16 July 2026**, as part of its summer agreement, the Brussels government appointed lawyer **Philippe Van Roost** as special commissioner of **Foyer anderlechtois**, for a period of **4 months**, tasked with taking over the governance of the company: convening and chairing the management bodies, validating submitted documents, and authorising the signing of deeds in place of the chair and vice-chair.

The following day, **Friday 17 July 2026**, in its last plenary session before the summer recess, the Brussels Parliament approved the **26 recommendations** of its inquiry committee on Foyer anderlechtois by **51 votes in favour**, **24 votes against** (PTB, Ecolo, DéFI, N-VA) and **6 abstentions**.

## Background: a scandal that broke in May 2026

The affair began with the broadcast, on 20 May 2026, of a report by the investigative programme Pano (VRT) documenting suspicions of favouritism in the allocation of social housing at Foyer anderlechtois. The Brussels public prosecutor's office opened an investigation into potential corruption as early as 21 May, followed by searches on 28 May. A parliamentary inquiry committee, set up on 5 June, held 143 hours of hearings with 50 witnesses before formulating its recommendations.
