## Context: exit from the provisional twelfths regime on the Cocom side

Since late 2024, several Brussels-dependent federated entities were operating under the **provisional twelfths regime**: month-by-month rollover of the previous budget, without the possibility of enacting new policy measures. Cause: the absence of a durably installed Brussels government and a stable parliamentary majority.

The **Cocom (Common Community Commission / GGC)** is the bi-community body competent for **persons** (not institutions) in matters of social policy, health, family and assistance to persons with disabilities. It funds and oversees notably **Iriscare**, **Vivalis** (administration), **Famiris** (family allowances) and **Samusocial**.

## Vote of 22 May 2026

On **Friday 22 May 2026**, the Brussels Parliament (in its **Cocom / GGC** configuration) adopts the **2026 budget** of the Common Community Commission, lifting a carencies regime that had lasted **seventeen months**.

**Key figures (announced ahead of the vote, government communication of 11 May 2026):**

- **Expected 2026 Cocom deficit:** EUR 99.7 M (EUR 35 M net after historical under-execution offsets).
- **Balance target:** 2029.
- **Planned cumulative savings:** at least EUR 11 M per year.
- **Iriscare / Vivalis staff:** no reductions planned.
- **Social envelopes:** maintained.

## Institutional impact

- The Cocom budget vote **complements** the Brussels regional budget vote of **27 March 2026** (53/32): across Brussels's three budgets (Region, Cocof, Cocom), Brussels is gradually exiting the provisional twelfths regime.
- The adoption enables **normal funding** of social operators dependent on Cocom, and in particular of the **family allowances** paid by **Famiris**.
- The **Court of Audit** had previously flagged the absence of concrete policy measures translated in the draft: an attention point for execution monitoring.
- Expected institutional step; to be watched on the **Cocof** (French Community Commission) side to see whether it also passes its 2026 budget or extends the transitional regime.
