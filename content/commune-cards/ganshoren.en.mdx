## A small municipality with good practices

Ganshoren is a small municipality of 25,500 inhabitants led by Jean-Paul Van Laethem (Les Engagés) in coalition with the PS. Despite its modest size, it achieves a good transparency score.

## Transparency: 5 out of 6 criteria

The **budget** and council **minutes** (dedicated page with agendas) are published online, the council is broadcast via **livestream** (YouTube and social media since 2020), the **mandate registry** is accessible, and the municipality uses **Fluicity** for **citizen participation** with a participatory budget. No **Open Data** portal.

## Citizen participation: Fluicity

Ganshoren launched its citizen participation platform via **Fluicity**, including a participatory budget allowing residents to propose and vote on municipal projects.

## Budget tensions and political conflict

The **property tax** was raised by 20% (3,240 → **3,880 additional centimes**). This increase provoked the anger of the opposition: **Open-MR** councillors walked out of the municipal council, calling the majority "dictators". **Paid parking** has been extended to new neighbourhoods.

## Avenue Broustin: end of the blockage, permit issued (18 May 2026)

After years of blockage, an urban planning permit was issued on **18 May 2026** for the redevelopment of Avenue Broustin, on the border of Ganshoren and Jette: removal of the concrete blocks, two reopened access points, redevelopment of two squares, and safety improvements at a junction ranked among the 40 most accident-prone in the Region. Mayor Jean-Paul Van Laethem welcomed the fact that "Ganshoren insisted from the start of the project on minimising the impact on residents' parking, and we were heard".

## Les Bruyères school: reconstruction contract awarded

The "Design & Build" contract for the reconstruction of Les Bruyères school (Clos Jacques Wagner) was awarded to the Hooyberghs / Brebuild / POLO Architecten consortium: 4 nursery classrooms and 12 primary classrooms across 3,710 m², for a cost of approximately EUR 9 million (including around EUR 5.3 million in subsidy from the Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles). Works are planned for summer 2028, lasting 18 months, with delivery targeted for the 2030 school year.

Sources: DH, La Libre, BX1 (18 May 2026, Avenue Broustin); DH (9 April 2026, Les Bruyères school).
