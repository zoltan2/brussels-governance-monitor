## Four decisions in one council of ministers

On **Thursday 11 June 2026**, the Brussels council of ministers adopts a set of decisions of unusual scope:

1. **Vivaqua** — decision in principle to inject **180 million EUR** into the intermunicipal water utility: the Region becomes a **49% shareholder** and obtains for the first time a seat on the board of directors. Hydria transfers five storm basins and 40 km of collector sewers to Vivaqua (see Vivaqua dossier).
2. **Shared e-scooters** — ban on free-floating electric scooters from **1 January 2027**; the licences of Bolt and Dott expire at the end of 2026 and the next public tender will exclude them (see Shared Mobility dossier).
3. **LEZ** — **first-reading** approval of two exemption scenarios for beneficiaries of the increased reimbursement status (BIM), sent to the Council of State and the Data Protection Authority (see LEZ dossier).
4. **RNP-07 flight route** — introduction of a **cease-and-desist action** against the federal State over the intensive use of the approach route to Brussels Airport, with possible penalty payments (see Climate card).

## Social Summit the same day

On the same 11 June, the government and the social partners gathered within Brupartners (the Brussels social-partner council) sign a **socio-economic protocol agreement** setting **17 shared priorities** for the legislature, around seven axes. Each minister must set up a steering committee by September 2026.

## Institutional reading

Four months after taking office, the government strings together decisions that touch on three commitments of the government agreement (financial consolidation of Vivaqua, LEZ reform, social dialogue) and opens a new contentious front with the federal level over flights above Brussels. The concentration of these decisions in a single council of ministers comes at a time when the majority is under pressure on other matters (social housing scandals, parliamentary inquiry committee, supervision over Saint-Josse).
